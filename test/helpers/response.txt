X</b> is an American <a href=\"/wiki/Progressive_metal\" title=\"Progressive metal\">progressive metal</a><sup id=\"cite_ref-1\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-1\">[1]</a></sup><sup id=\"cite_ref-2\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-2\">[2]</a></sup> band from <a href=\"/wiki/Middletown,_New_Jersey\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Middletown, New Jersey\">Middletown, New Jersey</a>. Founded in 1994, the band consists of guitarist <a href=\"/wiki/Michael_Romeo\" title=\"Michael Romeo\">Michael Romeo</a>, keyboardist <a href=\"/wiki/Michael_Pinnella\" title=\"Michael Pinnella\">Michael Pinnella</a>, drummer <a href=\"/wiki/Jason_Rullo\" title=\"Jason Rullo\">Jason Rullo</a>, lead vocalist <a href=\"/wiki/Russell_Allen\" title=\"Russell Allen\">Russell Allen</a> and bassist <a href=\"/wiki/Michael_Lepond\" title=\"Michael Lepond\">Michael Lepond</a>.</p>\n<p>Their albums <i><a href=\"/wiki/The_Divine_Wings_of_Tragedy\" title=\"The Divine Wings of Tragedy\">The Divine Wings of Tragedy</a></i> and <i><a href=\"/wiki/V-The_New_Mythology_Suite\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"V-The New Mythology Suite\">V: The New Mythology Suite</a></i> have given the band considerable attention within the progressive metal community. They also have achieved more commercial success with the 2007 album <i><a href=\"/wiki/Paradise_Lost_(Symphony_X_album)\" title=\"Paradise Lost (Symphony X album)\">Paradise Lost</a></i> and the 2011 album <i><a href=\"/wiki/Iconoclast_(Symphony_X_album)\" title=\"Iconoclast (Symphony X album)\">Iconoclast</a></i>, which reached number 76 on the <a href=\"/wiki/Billboard_200\" title=\"Billboard 200\">Billboard 200</a>. Romeo explained the band's name in a 2008 interview; <i>\"So, you know, the music we were coming up with had the keyboard thing and the guitar, and some classical elements, so the word ‘Symphony’ came up and, so the dramatic thing, you know? So then, somebody said ‘Symphony X’ and we were like, ‘yeah, that’s cool’ it is that kinda thing, and the ‘X’ is the unknown and all the other stuff we do.\"</i><sup id=\"cite_ref-3\" href=\"/w/index.php?title=Symphony_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1\" title=\"Edit section: History\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"Symphony_X_and_The_Damnation_Game_.281994.E2.88.9295.29\"><i>Symphony X</i> and <i>The Damnation Game</i> (1994−95)</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Symphony_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2\" title=\"Edit section: Symphony X and The Damnation Game (1994−95)\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In early 1994 <a href=\"/wiki/Michael_Romeo\" title=\"Michael Romeo\">Michael Romeo</a> (formerly of the bands Phantom's Opera and Gemini) recorded a studio album titled <i><a href=\"/wiki/The_Dark_Chapter\" title=\"The Dark Chapter\">The Dark Chapter</a></i>, which featured himself and keyboard player <a href=\"/wiki/Michael_Pinnella\" title=\"Michael Pinnella\">Michael Pinnella</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-alloutguitar1_4-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-alloutguitar1-4\">[4]</a></sup> The tape attracted a fair share of attention, particularly in <a href=\"/wiki/Japan\" title=\"Japan\">Japan</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-alloutguitar1_4-1\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-alloutguitar1-4\">[4]</a></sup> Romeo then recruited bassist <a href=\"/wiki/Thomas_Miller_(bassist)\" title=\"Thomas Miller (bassist)\">Thomas Miller</a>, drummer <a href=\"/wiki/Jason_Rullo\" title=\"Jason Rullo\">Jason Rullo</a>, and vocalist <a href=\"/wiki/Rod_Tyler\" class=\"mw-redirect\" title=\"Rod Tyler\">Rod Tyler</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-progmetalblog_5-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-progmetalblog-5\">[5]</a></sup> Their <a href=\"/wiki/Symphony_X_(album)\" title=\"Symphony X (album)\">self-titled debut album</a> was recorded in the same year, and released to a positive reception in Japan by the now-defunct record company Zero Corporation.<sup id=\"cite_ref-officialromeo_6-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-officialromeo-6\">[6]</a></sup> The band's second studio album, <i><a href=\"/wiki/The_Damnation_Game_(album)\" title=\"The Damnation Game (album)\">The Damnation Game</a></i>, followed six months later. Singer Rod Tyler had left the band by then and was replaced by current singer <a href=\"/wiki/Russell_Allen\" title=\"Russell Allen\">Russell Allen</a>.<sup id=\"cite_ref-FAQ-7_7-0\" class=\"reference\"><a href=\"#cite_note-FAQ-7-7\">[7]</a></sup></p>\n<h3><span class=\"mw-headline\" id=\"The_Divine_Wings_Of_Tragedy_and_Twilight_In_Olympus_.281996.E2.88.9298.29\"><i>The Divine Wings Of Tragedy</i> and <i>Twilight In Olympus</i> (1996−98)</span><span class=\"mw-editsection\"><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">[</span><a href=\"/w/index.php?title=Symphony_X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3\" title=\"Edit section: The Divine Wings Of Tragedy and Twilight In Olympus (1996−98)\">edit</a><span class=\"mw-editsection-bracket\">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><i><a href=\"/wiki/The_Divine_Wings_of_Tragedy\" title=\"The Divine Wings of Tragedy\">The Divine Wings of Tragedy</a></i> was the album that first gave Symphony X exposure in metal circles. It took a considerable time to record the album, with the recording sessions beginning in 1996 and concluding in 1997. The positive feedback from the specialized press served to establish Symphony X in <a href=\"/wiki/Europe\" title=\"Europe\">Europe</a>; their success in <a href=\"/wiki/Japan\" title=\"Japan\">Japan</a> only continued to grow.<sup class=\"noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact\" style=\"white-space:nowrap;\">[<i><a href=\"/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed\" title=\"Wikipedia:Citation needed\"><span title=\"This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)\">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>\n<p>At the end of 1997 and for part of the next year, Jason Rullo, their drummer, had to stay away from the band in order to solve personal issues. He was temporarily replaced by Thomas Walling. Even with a temporary drummer, the band recorded <i><a href=\"/wiki/Twilight_in_Olympus\" title=\"Twilight in Olympus\">Twilight in Olympus</a></i>, released in early 1998. That year also saw the first live performances from the band, which by then had already gathered many fans from around the globe. Their first official show happened in 1998, in <a href=\"/wiki/Japan\" title=\"Japan\">Japan</a>. It was soon followed by a world tour. Bassist Thomas Miller left the band and was replaced by <a href=\"/wiki/Michael_Lepond\" title=\"Michael Lepond\">Michael Lepond</a> in that year.</p>\n<p>A compilation album, <i><a href=\"/wiki/Prelude_to_the_Millennium\" title=\"Prelude to the Millennium\">Prelude to the Millennium</a></i>, was released by the end of 1998. The album features classics such as \"Communion and the Oracle\", \"Egypt\" and the mini-suite \"Rediscovery\", and includes pieces of classical music.</p>\n<p>The band went on a European and South American tour shortly after its release and recorded their first live album, <i><a href=\"/wiki/Live_on_the_Edge_of_Forever\" title=\"Live on the Edge of Forever\">Live on the Edge of Forever</a></i>.</p>\n<p>In 2002, the band released <i><a href=\"/wiki/The_Odyssey_(album)\" title=\"The Odyssey (album)\">The Odyssey</a></i>, an album prominently featuring a 24-minute-long musical interpretation of the <a href=\"/wiki/Homer\" title=\"Homer\">Homeric</a> <a href=\"/wiki/Epic_poetry\" title=\"Epic poetry\">epic</a>, the <a href=\"/wiki/Odyssey\" title=\"Odyssey\">Odyssey</a>.</p>\n<p>In 2005 Symphony X was featured on <a href=\"/wiki/Gigantour\" title=\"Gigantour\">Gigantour</a>, a summer festival headlined by <a href=\"/wiki/Megadeth\" title=\"Megadeth\">Megadeth</a> with <a href=\"/wiki/Dream_Theater\" title=\"Dream Theater\">Dream Theater</a>, <a href=\"/wiki/Nevermore\" title=\"Nevermore\">Nevermore</a> and <a href=\"/wiki/Anthrax_(American_band)\" title=\"Anthrax (American band)\">Anthrax</a>. During their show in London, England, the band debuted another new song titled \"Prometheus\".</p>\n<p>On February 27, 2013, it was announced that, during the previous week, drummer <a href=\"/wiki/Jason_Rullo\" title=\"Jason Rullo\">Jason Rullo</a> was admitted to hospital for heart failure.
